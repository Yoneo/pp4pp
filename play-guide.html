2) Am I required by the Google Play Store to post a privacy policy?

  1. For now (posted 13.6.2013) this is a resounding "no". The Play store does not require you in their terms to add a privacy policy. You can therefore post an app to the store that is non-compliant with privacy laws. From the Google Play docs: "This is an optional field that Google provides to developers who want to share their privacy policy with users - these privacy policies are written by the app developers and are not evaluated by Google". This does not however excempt you from any of your obligations.
  2. The California Attorney General is working on making all apps privacy regulations compliant and working on this with the big platform providers like Google Play. This situation could therefore change down the road.

Here's a document called Developer Privacy Policy for Android on the Google's support pages.

3) How do I add/edit my privacy policy on the Play store? (source)

  1. Log into your Google Play Developer Console
  2. Next, select All Applications and select the application whose privacy policy you'd like to edit.
  3. After that, select Store Listing.
  4. Then, scroll to the section marked Privacy Policy and enter the URL where you have the privacy policy hosted online.
  5. Lastly, be sure to click Save.
